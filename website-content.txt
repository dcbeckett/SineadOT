The practice provides evidence based Paediatric Occupational Therapy assessment and intervention.  In Occupational Therapy, the term Occupation refers to the everyday activities that are meaningful and important in a child’s life. For children, these occupations include play, learning, self-care, social interaction,rest and participation in family and community life. Occupational Therapy supports children to participate in these activities in ways that feel enjoyable, meaningful and supportive at every stage of their development. While recognising and honouring each individual child’s strengths, Sinead works collaboratively with families to build confidence in helping to support functional meaningful participation at home and school. We believe that every child deserves the opportunity to thrive and reach their full potential.

--- PAGE HEADER ---
Title: Services
Subtitle: Comprehensive Paediatric Occupational Therapy assessment and intervention tailored to your child's needs

--- INTRODUCTION ---
Our paediatric Occupational Therapy service is designed to help children develop essential skills, overcome developmental challenges, and participate fully in daily activities at home, school, and in the community.

--- MAIN SERVICES ---

Service 1: Assessment: The practice offers comprehensive Occupational Therapy assessment for children between the ages of 3-18. Assessment is completed using both standardised and unstandardised assessments. Together, these assessments provide a comprehensive understanding of your child’s needs, helping to guide intervention as required. Assessment includes looking at motor planning & co-ordination, daily living skills, sensory processing skills and school participation skills (inc hand writing).

Service 2: Intervention: Following assessment, intervention based sessions may be recommended. Using play-based and individualised approach, intervention is focused on helping your child build daily skills, confidence and independence. Listed below are some of the specific areas of difficulty we can help with. 



Service 1: Sensory Integration Therapy
Many children experience difficulties processing sensory information from their environment. Our sensory processing strategies help children who are over-responsive (sensory sensitive) or under-responsive (sensory seeking) to sensory input. Through play-based activities and structured interventions, we help children learn to regulate their sensory systems, improve attention and focus, reduce anxiety, and participate more fully in daily activities. We can also provide sensory strategies for home and school settings.

Service 2: Fine Motor Skills Development
Fine motor skills are essential for handwriting, drawing, cutting, buttoning, and many other daily tasks. We provide comprehensive assessments and targeted interventions to develop hand strength, finger dexterity, hand-eye coordination, and bilateral coordination. Our intervention based sessions include activities to improve pencil grasp, handwriting legibility, scissor skills, and manipulation of small objects. We can work closely with schools to support children's success in classroom activities.

Service 3: Self-Care and Daily Living Skills
Building independence in self-care activities is a crucial part of child development. We support children in developing skills for dressing, eating,  and other essential daily activities. Occupational Therapy can help address underlying skill deficits, provide adaptive strategies, and recommend appropriate equipment when needed. We work collaboratively with families to establish routines and strategies that promote independence at home and school.

Service 4: School Readiness and Academic Support
Preparing children for the demands of school requires a range of developmental skills. We assess and support pre-academic skills including attention, sitting tolerance, following instructions, visual-motor integration, and early literacy skills. For school-aged children, we address challenges with handwriting, organisation, attention in the classroom, and participation in school activities. We provide recommendations for classroom accommodations and can work collaboratively with teachers to support each child's success.

Service 5 :Gross Motor Skills and Coordination
Supporting children with balance, coordination, motor planning, and body awareness through movement-based activities and therapeutic exercises.
