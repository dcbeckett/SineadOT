Our Services

Comprehensive Paediatric Occupational Therapy assessment and intervention tailored to your child's needs

Our paediatric Occupational Therapy services are designed to help children develop essential skills, overcome developmental challenges, and participate fully in daily activities at home, school, and in the community.

Assessment

We offer comprehensive Occupational Therapy assessment for children between the ages of 3-18. Together, these assessments provide a comprehensive understanding of your child's needs, helping to guide intervention as required. Assessment includes looking at motor planning & co-ordination, daily living skills, sensory processing skills and school participation skills (inc hand writing).

Intervention

Following assessment, intervention based sessions may be recommended. Using play-based and individualised approach, intervention is focused on helping your child build daily skills, confidence and independence. Listed below are some of the specific areas of difficulty we can help with.

Sensory Integration Therapy

Many children experience difficulties processing sensory information from their environment. Our sensory processing strategies help children who are over-responsive (sensory sensitive) or under-responsive (sensory seeking) to sensory input. Through play-based activities and structured interventions, we help children regulate their sensory systems, improve attention and focus, reduce anxiety, and participate more fully in daily activities. We can also provide sensory strategies for home and school settings.

Fine Motor Skills Development

Fine motor skills are essential for handwriting, drawing, cutting, buttoning, and many other daily tasks. We provide comprehensive assessments and targeted interventions to develop hand strength, finger dexterity, hand-eye coordination, and bilateral coordination. Our therapy includes activities to improve pencil grasp, handwriting legibility, scissor skills, and manipulation of small objects. We work closely with schools to support children's success in classroom activities.

Self-Care and Daily Living Skills

Building independence in self-care activities is a crucial part of child development. We support children in developing skills for dressing, eating, grooming, toileting, and other essential daily activities. Our interventions address underlying skill deficits, provide adaptive strategies, and recommend appropriate equipment when needed. We work collaboratively with families to establish routines and strategies that promote independence at home and school.

School Readiness and Academic Support

Preparing children for the demands of school requires a range of developmental skills. We assess and support pre-academic skills including attention, sitting tolerance, following instructions, visual-motor integration, and early literacy skills. For school-aged children, we address challenges with handwriting, organisation, attention in the classroom, and participation in school activities. We provide recommendations for classroom accommodations and work collaboratively with teachers to support each child's success.

Gross Motor Skills and Coordination

Supporting children with balance, coordination, motor planning, and body awareness through movement-based activities and therapeutic exercises.
